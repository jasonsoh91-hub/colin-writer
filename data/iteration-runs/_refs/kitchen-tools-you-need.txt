# kitchen tools you need

You can spend hundreds on the latest kitchen gadget if you’d like. Or you can spend a fraction of that on a few deceptively simple tools that you’ll end up reaching for almost every day. When people start taking cooking more seriously, there’s a tendency to assume the next step is buying more stuff. A stand mixer. A food processor. Perhaps one of those kitchen gadgets with a suspiciously specific purpose that somehow occupies half a cupboard despite being used twice a year. In reality, some of the most transformative kitchen upgrades are remarkably humble. They don’t come with touchscreens, apps, or enough buttons to pilot a small aircraft. They’re just simple tools that solve annoyingly common problems better than anything else. Here are three that’ll really make cooking a breeze. Photography: epicurious | Instagram Danish dough whisk At first glance, a Danish dough whisk looks less like a kitchen utensil and more like something an eccentric inventor put together to conduct electricity. It’s essentially a wooden handle attached to a series of looping steel wires, and it doesn’t exactly inspire confidence. That is, until you use one. If you’ve ever tried mixing bread dough, cookie dough, or a particularly thick pancake batter with a conventional whisk, you’ll be familiar with the struggle. The wires clog instantly, turning the whisk into a flour-coated lump, and a wooden spoon fares little better, requiring enough effort that your forearm starts cursing you out halfway through. With the Danish dough whisk, though, you can sidestep all of this. Its open loops cut through thick mixtures with surprising ease, incorporating ingredients quickly without having the whole affair double as an upper-body workout. It also happens to be oddly satisfying to use, and that has to count for something. Photography: toadfish.com Fish spatula Despite the name, the fish spatula might just be the best all-purpose spatula ever made. Originally designed for handling delicate fish fillets without tearing them apart, its thin, flexible blade makes it remarkably adept at everything else too. It slips effortlessly under eggs, pancakes, burgers, and just about anything else you’d care to cook in a pan. With most spatulas, it’s somewhat of a choice between strength and finesse. The fish spatula somehow, in a show of cooking utensil excellence, manages both. It’s sturdy enough to lift heavier foods, yet flexible enough to slide beneath fragile ones with minimal fuss. Get one today and never have to duel your pan for your food ever again. Photography: Christina Xu | Flickr Digital thermometer Picture this: you’ve been roasting your chicken for what seems like eons now. Your stomach is growling loud enough for the neighbours to start banging on the wall, and you need to get a forkful in you right this second or you might just physically expire. You cut into the chicken, and to your shock, horror, and dismay, it’s dry enough to qualify as building material. The problem is that most of us are surprisingly bad at judging doneness by sight alone. We poke things, prod things, squint at them suspiciously, and then hope for the best. A digital thermometer removes this guesswork entirely. Whether it be chicken, steak, fish, or even bread, knowing the exact internal temperature gives you a level of confidence that recipes alone could never provide. It transforms cooking from educated guesswork into something considerably more predictable, and more importantly, it prevents those moments where an expensive cut of meat becomes an expensive chew toy instead that even the dog thinks is not worth the bother. The next time you’re tempted by some bulky kitchen gadget promising to revolutionise your cooking, consider spending that money on a simple tool that performs one task exceptionally well instead. Chances are it’ll earn a permanent place in your kitchen long after the novelty of everything else has worn off.
